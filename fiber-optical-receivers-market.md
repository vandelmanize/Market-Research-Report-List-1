<p><p>The fiber optical receivers market includes various form factors catering to different data transmission needs. SFP (Small Form-factor Pluggable) and SFP+ support standard and enhanced speeds, respectively. QSFP/QSFP+ (Quad Small Form-factor Pluggable) handles higher data rates for aggregation applications. XFP (10 Gigabit Small Form-factor Pluggable) is designed for 10 Gbps networks. CFP (C Form-factor Pluggable) and CXP (C Form-factor Pluggable) provide higher capacity for ultra-high-speed applications, making them essential for data centers and high-performance computing environments.</p></p>
