<p><p>Photosensitive polyimides are key materials in electronic packaging, utilized for their excellent thermal stability and insulating properties. The market is divided into two types: positive photosensitive polyimides, which become soluble upon exposure to light, allowing for precise patterning in microfabrication processes; and negative photosensitive polyimides, which harden upon exposure, offering robust structural integrity. Both types facilitate the creation of intricate electronic components, enhancing the performance and reliability of modern electronic devices. Their applications span semiconductors, displays, and circuit boards.</p></p>
