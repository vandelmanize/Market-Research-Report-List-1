<p><p>Backside-illuminated (BSI) CMOS sensors are advanced imaging devices where the photodiode is placed on the backside, allowing light to pass through the silicon substrate before reaching the sensor. This design enhances light capture, resulting in improved sensitivity and performance in low-light conditions. BSI technology is increasingly utilized in smartphones, digital cameras, and other imaging applications due to its ability to produce high-quality images with reduced noise.</p><p>The BSI CMOS sensor market is witnessing significant growth, driven by the rising demand for high-resolution imaging in consumer electronics and the expanding applications in automotive, security, and surveillance systems. <p><p>The backside-illuminated (BSI) CMOS sensor market is primarily segmented into two types: Passive Pixel Sensor (PPS) CMOS and Active Pixel Sensor (APS) CMOS. PPS CMOS sensors utilize a simpler design with fewer components, leading to lower costs but limited performance, making them suitable for basic applications. In contrast, APS CMOS sensors incorporate multiple active components, offering higher image quality and enhanced performance, making them ideal for advanced imaging applications like smartphones and digital cameras where clarity and detail are crucial.</p></p>
